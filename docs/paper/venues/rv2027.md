# RV 2027 submission plan

**27th International Conference on Runtime Verification**

- **CFP status:** Not yet published (RV 2027 organising committee
  typically announces the CFP around April–May 2027 with submission
  deadlines in late June–early July).
- **Why not RV 2026:** the 2026 CFP closed 14 June 2026, three days
  after this artifact was finalised. Hard to peer-review-grade a
  paper in 72 hours. RV 2027 gives a full year of polishing,
  artifact-evaluation preparation, and (potentially) third-party
  reproductions.
- **Format:** LNCS Springer
- **Tracks of interest:** regular paper, tool paper, tool
  demonstration. The current paper draft would target the **tool
  paper track** (typically 6 pages) or the **regular paper track**
  (typically 16 pages) depending on space.
- **Artifact evaluation:** RV typically operates an artifact
  evaluation committee that reproduces submitted tools in a clean
  environment. The current Project Ghost CI matrix
  (Ubuntu + Windows × Python 3.11/3.12) and OIDC-signed PyPI wheel
  already provide this in production form.

## Why RV second (not first)

- **RV reviewers value depth of mechanism over breadth of
  framing.** Theorem 1 + the three TLA+ specs + the violation
  showcase are the load-bearing content; the autonomy-domain framing
  matters less than at FMAS.
- **Lead with the partition theorem and Theorem 1.** RV reviewers
  see TLA+ specs frequently for distributed algorithms but rarely
  for safety supervisors of autonomy; that's the novelty hook.
- **Tools track may be a better fit than regular paper.** The paper
  is a tool report at its core (Ghost the package + CLI + verifier
  algorithm); the formal contributions (C1, C2) support the tool's
  credibility rather than standing alone.

## Adaptation of `docs/paper/arxiv/main.tex` for RV 2027

Springer LNCS:

1. Download `llncs.cls` from
   <https://www.springer.com/gp/computer-science/lncs/conference-proceedings-guidelines>.
2. Copy `docs/paper/arxiv/main.tex` to `docs/paper/rv2027/main.tex`.
3. Replace the documentclass line:
   ```latex
   \documentclass[runningheads]{llncs}
   ```
4. Remove `\usepackage{geometry}` (LNCS sets its own margins).
5. Replace the title block with the LNCS form:
   ```latex
   \title{Project Ghost: A Verifiable Safety-Property Surface for Autonomy Under Uncertainty}
   \author{Javier Men\'endez Mateos\inst{1}}
   \institute{Independent\\\email{jfhelvetius@gmail.com}}
   ```
6. Trim to the venue's page limit. For a tool paper (~6 pages):
   condense §1.2, §2.1 (move to references), §9 (drop), and §6.4
   (operational tightness check) to a single paragraph. The proof
   of Theorem 1 may go to a separate technical report linked from
   the paper.

## Strengthening for RV 2027 specifically

- **Lead with Theorem 1 in the abstract.** RV reviewers respond to
  concrete formal results; the partition theorem and the closed-form
  bound are the strongest hooks.
- **Foreground the violation showcase** as a "the verifier works"
  empirical demonstration of value. RV cares deeply that runtime
  verifiers actually detect bugs.
- **Add a section on artifact reproducibility** matching the RV
  artifact evaluation criteria: Docker image (consider building one
  pre-submission), VM specification, run instructions, expected
  outputs hash-pinned.
- **Consider a separate technical-report companion** on arXiv with
  the full proofs (TLA+ source, Theorem 1 proof in full detail, all
  five property statements verbatim from the ADRs). The RV paper
  cites the report.
- **Comparison matrix (§2.3)** — RV reviewers will check this
  carefully. Verify RTAMT and MoonLight haven't introduced new
  features in 2026 that change the differentiation; the agent's
  prior-art report (June 2026) is the baseline.
