## Why FMAS first

- **Topical fit is exact.** FMAS is "formal methods for autonomous
  systems"; the paper is "formal safety properties + mechanical
  verification of an autonomy supervisor". The C2 partition theorem
  + Theorem 1 + the three TLA+ specs land on exactly the workshop's
  thematic centre.
- **Length friendly.** EPTCS papers are typically 12–15 pages; the
  current LaTeX draft (~12 pages) fits without trimming.
- **Workshop culture rewards reproducibility.** The end-to-end
  citation pattern (C4) — `pip install project-ghost==0.2.1` +
  re-runnable verifier — is unusual at the workshop and a
  differentiator.

## Adaptation of `docs/paper/arxiv/main.tex` for FMAS

EPTCS provides `eptcs.cls` at <https://eptcs.web.cse.unsw.edu.au/>
(`eptcs.cls` plus `eptcsalpha.bst`). Steps:

1. Download `eptcsstyle.zip` from the EPTCS site; extract `eptcs.cls`
   and `eptcsalpha.bst` into `docs/paper/fmas2026/`.
2. Copy `docs/paper/arxiv/main.tex` to `docs/paper/fmas2026/main.tex`.
3. Replace the documentclass line:
   ```latex
   \documentclass[submission,copyright,creativecommons]{eptcs}
   ```
4. Remove `\usepackage{geometry}` (EPTCS sets its own margins).
5. Replace the title block:
   ```latex
   \title{Project Ghost: A Verifiable Safety-Property Surface for Autonomy Under Uncertainty}
   \author{Javier Men\'endez Mateos
       \institute{Independent}
       \email{jfhelvetius@gmail.com}
   }
   ```
6. Replace `\bibliographystyle{plainurl}` with
   `\bibliographystyle{eptcsalpha}`.
7. Compile: `pdflatex main; bibtex main; pdflatex main; pdflatex main`.
8. Verify the page count: EPTCS workshop papers are typically 12–15
   pages including references. The current draft is ~12 — leave
   room to expand §1 motivation (FMAS values autonomy-domain framing)
   and trim §9 future work if needed.

## Strengthening for FMAS specifically

FMAS reviewers weight autonomy-context motivation higher than RV
reviewers do. Targeted edits before submission:

- **Expand §1 first paragraph** with a concrete autonomy-failure
  vignette (e.g., GPS-denied drone with calibrated overconfidence).
  Currently the motivation is generic; FMAS prefers domain-specific
  stakes.
- **Add a paragraph in §7 (Reproducibility surface)** explicitly
  reading the citation pattern as a *response to the safety-claim
  reproducibility crisis* that simulation videos and prose ADRs
  cannot solve.
- **Highlight Theorem 1's "structural sanity" reading** (Corollary
  2): the bound is also a *malfunction detector* for the sliding
  window, not only a recovery latency guarantee. FMAS reviewers
  appreciate dual readings.
- **Consider supplementary artifact appendix** with the full
  `closed_loop_smoke_violated.py` source + TLA+ specs in an
  appendix (extra pages are typically allowed for appendices in
  EPTCS).
